The most commonly known greenhouse gases is carbon dioxide (CO2), a colourless and odourless gas that is released when fossil fuels are burnt. The carbon in the carbon dioxide will be stored for millions of years under the ground.

After carbon dioxide, the next most abundant greenhouse gas is methane (CH4). Although methane is shorter-lived in the atmosphere than carbon dioxide, methane traps roughly 30 times more heat than carbon dioxide. This makes methane a gas that is even more hazadous to our environment than carbon dioxide.

The beef and dairy industry is one of the main contributors to methane. What cows eat has a big effect on how much methane they emit. Cows produces methane in two main ways: through digestion and through waste. Digesting certain types of food produces more methane than digesting other foods.

Methane gas emissions from livestock are a huge problem. Despite our countless efforts to drive less, save electricity, recycle, it may not be the most effective to mitigate climate change. Experts now believe that eating less meat would actually have a bigger impact.
